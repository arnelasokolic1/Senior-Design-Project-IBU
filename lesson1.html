What is Blockchain?

Overview

A blockchain is a shared, decentralized digital ledger that records transactions across many computers. Instead of relying on one central authority (like a bank), it allows data to be stored in a way that is transparent, tamper-resistant, and secure.

How Does Blockchain Work?

Data on a blockchain is stored in ‚Äúblocks.‚Äù Each block contains a group of transactions. Once a block is full, it is linked to the previous block using a unique identifier called a hash. This chain of blocks is where the name ‚Äúblockchain‚Äù comes from.

  • Transaction: Someone sends information or value (like cryptocurrency).
  • Verification: The network of computers checks that the transaction is valid.
  • Block Creation: Valid transactions are grouped into a block.
  • Linking: Each block includes the hash of the previous block, keeping the chain secure.

Key Features

  • Decentralization: No single company or person controls the system.
  • Transparency: Anyone can see the ledger of transactions.
  • Security: Changing old records would require rewriting the entire chain, which is almost impossible.
  • Immutability: Once data is recorded, it cannot easily be changed.

Real-World Uses of Blockchain

Although blockchain is best known as the technology behind Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies, it is used in many other areas:

  • Finance: Faster, cheaper international payments.
  • Supply Chain: Tracking goods from the factory to the store.
  • Healthcare: Securely sharing patient medical records.
  • Voting: Building tamper-proof electronic voting systems.
  • NFTs & Digital Ownership: Proving ownership of digital art, music, and collectibles.

Advantages and Challenges

  • Advantages: Secure, transparent, resistant to censorship, reduces middlemen.
  • Challenges: High energy use (for Proof of Work), scalability issues, regulatory uncertainty.
